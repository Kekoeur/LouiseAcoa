Questions & answers

At what stage of a project do you usually intervene?

Interventions may occur at different stages: early strategic evaluation, project structuring, criticaldecision points or during operational execution.

Are you willing to work several days per week on a project?

Yes. Depending on the project's needs, ACOA can support organizations several days per week over several months, helping teams structure and secure complex initiatives.

Do you work alone ?

The offer “monthly support” is indeed an offer where I would provide support alone but I can bring in my network for complementary support if needed. In that case, contracts are made directly with my partners, with no extra fee.

On specific projects, I prefer to work within a team, acting as a reference person to review facts & figures, identify constraints, propose solutions, ideas and support implementation.

Do you work directly with project teams?

Yes. ACOA works alongside leadership and operational teams, helping clarify priorities, structuredecisions and maintain alignment throughout the project.

Are you available immediately?

Yes. Éric is known for his very fast response time, allowing him to intervene immediately.

Would you consider a one-day mission?

Yes. Short interventions bring significant value to clarify a situation or support a strategic decision.

Are you available full-time to replace a project manager temporarily?

Not on a full-time basis. ACOA's role is to provide experienced advisory and support, strengthening teams rather than replacing internal roles.

Do you work with consulting firms?

Yes. Consulting teams sometimes request ACOA's support to industrial or operational challenges
